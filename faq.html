FAQ – About This Test

What is this test?

This test is a fun and personal way to explore different interests and dynamics. There are no right or wrong results. It’s meant for curiosity, self-reflection, and entertainment only.

What does BDSM mean?

BDSM is a broad term used to describe different types of consensual role-play, power exchange, and personal expression. The word comes from three parts:

  • Bondage & Discipline – rules, structure, training, or physical restraint
  • Dominance & Submission – power exchange, control, obedience, or service
  • Sadism & Masochism – enjoying giving or receiving pain in agreed situations

These activities are always based on consent, trust, and clear boundaries. That is what separates BDSM from abuse.

What are BDSM roles or archetypes?

Everyone has different preferences. Most people don’t fit perfectly into one role. These labels simply help describe common patterns. You may relate to some, a little, a lot, or not at all.

What is an Ageplayer?

Ageplayers explore age as part of role-play. This may include acting younger or older than their real age, using behavior or items that match the role. It is about mindset and role-play, not real life.

What is a Brat?

A brat is a playful type of submissive who enjoys teasing or resisting in a fun way. This behavior is usually part of the dynamic and done with understanding on both sides.

What is a Brat Tamer?

A brat tamer enjoys handling playful resistance. They don’t take the teasing personally and often see it as part of the connection.

What does Daddy or Mommy mean here?

These roles focus on care, guidance, and protection combined with authority. It often includes emotional support and structure, not just control.

What is a Dominant?

Dominants enjoy leading or being in charge. Some prefer full control, others enjoy negotiation or playful resistance. This can exist only in private moments or as part of daily life.

What is a Submissive?

Submissives enjoy following or giving up control in agreed situations. This doesn’t mean weakness. It’s about trust and choice.

What is a Switch?

Switches enjoy both dominant and submissive roles depending on mood, partner, or situation.

What is a Masochist or Sadist?

Masochists enjoy receiving certain kinds of pain, while sadists enjoy giving it. Pain tolerance is different for everyone and is always discussed beforehand.

What is a Master, Mistress, or Slave?

These roles involve deeper power exchange and responsibility. They often extend beyond single moments and are built on strong trust and rules.

What is Pet or Owner play?

Pet play focuses on care, structure, and role-play. It may or may not involve sexuality. Some people enjoy the emotional comfort and simplicity of the role.

What does Primal mean?

Primal play focuses on instinct, emotion, and raw energy rather than strict rules. It’s about natural reactions and intensity.

What does Vanilla mean?

Vanilla describes people who enjoy more traditional relationships or activities. There is nothing wrong with that. Enjoyment is what matters.

What are Voyeurs and Exhibitionists?

Voyeurs enjoy watching, while exhibitionists enjoy being seen. Both are based on consent and comfort.

What is an Experimentalist?

Experimentalists are curious and open-minded. They like exploring new ideas and experiences before forming opinions.

Is my data saved?

No. Your answers are not stored or tracked. Everything happens locally in your browser.

Is there an age limit?

This site is intended for adults only. If you are under 18, please do not use it.

Frequently Asked Questions

My results don’t feel right or I’m not happy with them

This usually happens when a question is read differently than it was intended, or when your personal understanding of an archetype doesn’t fully match the description used in the test. That’s completely okay. You know yourself better than any test ever could, and you’re free to interpret your results in a way that feels right to you.

Can you send me my original answers?

No, we can’t do that. For privacy reasons, individual answers are not stored in a way that allows them to be retrieved. Since anyone can enter a result using just a result ID, there’s also no reliable way to confirm ownership of a specific result.

Your overall scores will always remain accessible using your result ID, but the individual answers themselves cannot be recovered.

If anything feels unclear, you can always reach out for help.

I have an old screenshot without a result ID. Can I recover my result?

Unfortunately, no. Without the result ID, there’s no practical way to locate a specific result. There are a very large number of results in the system, and searching by score alone isn’t possible.

Can I feature this test on my website or use it in a project or research?

Yes, absolutely. You’re welcome to reference or feature the test without asking for permission. We’re always happy to see it shared.

If you need formal written consent for something like academic research, film production, or official documentation, you can request it by email.

Who created this test?

It was created by people from the community, for the community. The authors have chosen to remain anonymous, as the focus of the project is on providing a free and helpful tool rather than personal recognition.

From time to time, people may claim to be the creators, but those claims are incorrect. None of the original authors have publicly identified themselves.
